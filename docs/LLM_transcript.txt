In requirements engineering, an invariant is a condition or property that must always hold true for a system, regardless of state, time, or execution path.

Think of it as a non-negotiable rule of the system.

Plain-English definition

An invariant is a constraint that the system must never violate.

If the invariant is ever false, the system is incorrect, no matter what feature is being used.

Why invariants matter in requirements engineering

Invariants help you:

Precisely define system correctness

Catch contradictions or missing requirements early

Serve as a bridge between requirements, design, and verification

Provide a basis for formal verification and testing
